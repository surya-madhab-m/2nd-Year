S=vit+1/2at2:The Equation of Linear Motion

The formula S=vit+1/2at2 describes the motion of an object under constant acceleration.

  • S represents displacement
  • vi is the initial velocity
  • t is the time
  • a is the acceleration
  • 1/2at2 is the additional displacement due to acceleration

    • "An object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force."
    Note: This equation was originally written in 1905.
